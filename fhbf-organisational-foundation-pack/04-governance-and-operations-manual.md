# FHBF Governance & Operations Manual

## Purpose

This manual explains how FHBF should be structured and operated.

## Governance Structure

### Patron
Provides spiritual covering, wisdom and strategic guidance.

### Advisory Board
Provides professional advice and credibility. Members may come from business, finance, law, education, property, agriculture, technology and healthcare.

### Executive Committee
Responsible for day-to-day strategic leadership.

### Working Committees
Support specific areas of FHBF operations.

## Executive Roles

### President
Provides leadership, represents FHBF and oversees strategy.

### Vice President
Supports the President and assists with coordination.

### Secretary General
Manages records, meetings, minutes and correspondence.

### Treasurer
Oversees finance, budgets, reporting and controls.

### Membership Director
Leads recruitment, applications, renewals and member care.

### Events Director
Oversees Business Village, forums, breakfasts and conferences.

### Sponsorship Director
Leads sponsor recruitment and partner management.

### Trade & Investment Director
Develops investor networks, trade missions and business tours.

### Media & Communications Director
Manages website, newsletter, social media and publicity.

### Technology Director
Oversees website, database, member portal and digital systems.

### Academy Director
Develops training, mentorship and learning programmes.

## Committees

### Membership & Growth
Recruit members, review applications and support retention.

### Events & Business Village
Plan and manage all FHBF events.

### Sponsorship & Partnerships
Engage sponsors, corporates and institutions.

### Trade & Investment
Coordinate trade missions, investor forums and business tours.

### Academy & Mentorship
Develop learning pathways and mentor matching.

### Media & Communications
Promote FHBF and member stories.

### Technology & Innovation
Develop digital platforms and innovation strategy.

### Prayer & Community Impact
Support prayer, testimonies and impact programmes.

## Meeting Rhythm

- Executive Committee: monthly
- Working Committees: monthly or as required
- Advisory Board: quarterly
- General Members Meeting: annually
- Event planning meetings: weekly near major events

## Financial Controls

Recommended controls:
- Two authorised signatories.
- Clear budget approval.
- Written receipts.
- Payment tracking.
- Monthly finance report.
- Annual financial summary.
- Separation of duties where possible.

## Records

FHBF should maintain:
- Membership register.
- Applications register.
- Payment register.
- Business directory records.
- Event registrations.
- Sponsor agreements.
- Meeting minutes.
- Annual reports.
