# Faith Heights Business Fellowship Constitution Draft

## 1. Name

The name of the organisation shall be Faith Heights Business Fellowship, abbreviated as FHBF.

## 2. Identity

FHBF is a Kingdom business fellowship connected to the Faith Heights vision. It exists to connect entrepreneurs, professionals, business owners, investors, institutions and leaders for enterprise, growth, impact and Kingdom influence.

## 3. Vision

To create a thriving Kingdom business ecosystem that empowers entrepreneurs, professionals, investors, organisations and communities to connect, grow, trade, invest, learn and positively influence society through ethical and faith-driven enterprise.

## 4. Mission

FHBF shall:
- Provide a platform for business networking and collaboration.
- Promote integrity, excellence and stewardship in business.
- Support entrepreneurship and enterprise development.
- Create opportunities for members to access markets, customers, investors and partners.
- Host business events, exhibitions, forums, trade missions and training programmes.
- Promote member businesses through directories, media and digital platforms.
- Encourage prayer, mentorship and community impact among business leaders.

## 5. Objectives

The objectives of FHBF are to:
1. Establish a structured fellowship for Kingdom-minded business people.
2. Create a trusted member business directory.
3. Organise the Faith Heights Business Village and other business events.
4. Develop training and mentorship programmes.
5. Facilitate trade missions, business tours and exposure opportunities.
6. Promote investment, procurement and partnership opportunities.
7. Encourage ethical business practice and accountability.
8. Support youth entrepreneurship, women in business and community development.
9. Build local, national and international business chapters.

## 6. Membership Classes

FHBF may admit the following members:
- Student Member
- Entrepreneur Member
- Professional Member
- Business Member
- Corporate Partner
- Sponsor Partner
- Honorary Member
- Founding Member

## 7. Membership Admission

Membership shall be by application, review and approval. The Executive Committee or authorised Membership Committee may approve, decline or request further information.

## 8. Membership Responsibilities

Members shall:
- Uphold the values of FHBF.
- Conduct business ethically.
- Avoid misrepresentation or fraudulent activity.
- Respect other members.
- Pay applicable membership fees.
- Maintain accurate profile and directory information.
- Protect the reputation of FHBF.

## 9. Governance

FHBF shall be governed by:
- Patron
- Advisory Board
- Executive Committee
- Working Committees
- Chapter Committees where applicable

## 10. Executive Committee

The Executive Committee may include:
- President
- Vice President
- Secretary General
- Treasurer
- Membership Director
- Events Director
- Sponsorship Director
- Trade & Investment Director
- Media & Communications Director
- Technology Director
- Academy Director

## 11. Committees

FHBF may establish committees including:
- Membership & Growth Committee
- Events & Business Village Committee
- Sponsorship & Partnerships Committee
- Trade & Investment Committee
- Academy & Mentorship Committee
- Media & Communications Committee
- Technology & Innovation Committee
- Prayer & Community Impact Committee

## 12. Meetings

FHBF may hold:
- Annual General Meeting
- Executive Committee meetings
- Special meetings
- Chapter meetings
- Member networking meetings

## 13. Finances

FHBF income may include:
- Membership fees
- Business Village fees
- Sponsorships
- Donations
- Event fees
- Trade mission fees
- Academy/course fees
- Directory and advertising fees

All funds shall be managed transparently and used to advance the objectives of FHBF.

## 14. Discipline

FHBF may suspend or terminate membership where a member:
- Engages in fraud or unethical conduct.
- Brings FHBF into disrepute.
- Misuses the FHBF platform.
- Fails to comply with the code of conduct.
- Fails to pay fees after reasonable notice.

## 15. Amendments

This constitution may be amended by FHBF leadership according to an agreed governance process.

## 16. Dissolution

If FHBF is dissolved, remaining assets shall be applied toward Kingdom, charitable, educational or community development purposes as determined by the authorised leadership.
